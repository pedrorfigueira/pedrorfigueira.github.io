Pedro Figueira’s Astronomy

Glad to have you here! Explore my research, instrumentation work, publications, and science communication projects.

Research

My scientific interests lie mainly in exoplanets: their detection, their dynamical evolution and their formation. I am especially focused on the radial velocity detection of exoplanets, and improving the precision and the techniques used to trace ever smaller signals.

I am deeply involved in the ESPRESSO GTO science consortium, participating in the NCORES and LCE science programs. I have co-led the discovery of several exoplanets, and contributed to characterizing some of the most precise radial-velocity instruments ever built.

Instrumentation

I have contributed to multiple high-resolution spectrograph projects, including ESPRESSO, NIRPS, and HIRES. My work focuses on calibration sources, spectrograph stabilization, and data-reduction pipelines. I am particularly interested in improving precision for exoplanet detection.

Science Communication

I believe scientific outreach is a fundamental responsibility of the scientific community. I actively participate in public lectures, media interviews, podcasts, and school outreach programs, aiming to bring astronomy and exoplanetary science to the general public.

Helios

Helios is a personal project aimed at organizing research material, code, and ideas relating to exoplanet detection and instrumentation. It is a growing repository of scientific tools and educational resources.
